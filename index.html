Greetings and welcome to my GitHub page! I earned my bachelor's degree in Actuarial Sciences and Financial Mathematics from the School of Mathematical and Computational Sciences at the University of Prince Edward Island. Building on this foundation, I began my Master of Mathematics (MMath) in Computational Mathematics at the University of Waterloo in September 2022.

I am pleased to announce the successful defense and submission of my Master's research paper, marking the completion of my academic journey at the University of Waterloo. With my studies concluded, I seek opportunities to leverage my skills and knowledge in data science, data analysis, and insurance modeling (climate and weather catastrophe).

I am passionate about combining data science and computational methods to solve real-world problems in finance, insurance, and climate analytics. I also enjoy teaching and mentoring students while contributing to impactful research projects.

Research Experience

Finance and Actuarial Research:

  • I helped develop a new risk measure for buy-and-hold and delta hedge portfolios, using a geometrical approach and thresholds.
  • I constructed ARIMA time series models to accurately forecast insurance reimbursements for prescribed medications, presenting findings through an interactive RShiny dashboard.

Climate Change Research:

  • I spearheaded a project at the Canadian Centre of Climate Change and Adaptation, conducting an in-depth analysis of domestic and international climate change policies. This involved utilizing advanced methodologies such as machine learning, regression models, and similarity metrics to assess the synergy among G7 countries in achieving Sustainable Development Goals.
  • My Master's research introduced a groundbreaking Loss Layering model, enhancing insurers' risk management by numerically retaining specific loss layers. This approach enabled precise risk classification and strategic asset allocation, bolstering resilience against evolving climate patterns and, at the same time, reducing the amount of risk for insurers.
