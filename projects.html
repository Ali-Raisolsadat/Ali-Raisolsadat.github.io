Publications

  • Quantitative Assessment of The Group of Seven's Collaboration in Sustainable Development Goals
    (DOI: 10.1038/s41467-024-51663-5)
    I'm thrilled to share that my first paper has been published in Nature Communications! After dedicating almost 3 years to this research, it's incredibly rewarding to see this work come to fruition.

    This paper introduces a synergy-based framework for quantitatively assessing international cooperation towards the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and a detailed breakdown of synergy contribution from individual countries. Our findings identify notable discrepancies and lack of cooperation of G7 countries in achieving environmental SDGs, emphasizing the need for enhanced collaboration to address critical issues like climate change and biodiversity loss.

Undergraduate Course Projects

  • Black-Scholes-Merton Model: This paper briefly introduces European options and derives the renowned Black-Scholes equation using a partial differential equation (PDE) heat equation in continuous time. It serves as the final paper for the third-year Mathematical Modelling course.
  • Exploring Machine Learning in Finance: This project delves into a machine learning algorithm that utilizes financial data from various companies to assess their financial health. It represents the culmination of the fourth-year Machine Learning and Data Mining course. Access the project's summary here.
  • Impact of sustainable goals on social welfare and resilience. This completed project for my fourth Data Mining and Visualization class defines an index designed to track the effects of policy changes on countries' welfare systems. It extensively employs complex Principal Component Analysis (PCA) techniques and is presented as part of a two-part series. The presentation is for the first part.
  • This simple script (GBM Option Pricing in Python) uses a portfolio construction approach to replicate the price of a European option using both a risky and risk-free asset. This strategy involves combining the risky and risk-free assets within a portfolio to simulate the payoff of the European option at expiration using Monte Carlo simulations of geometric Brownian motion. To view the PDF version this script please view here.
  • Similarly, the Binomial Option Pricing Model (Binomial Option Pricing in Python) can replicate the price of a European option using a risky asset and a risk-free asset under the risk-neutral pricing approach. (PDF version: Binomial Option Pricing In Python - PDF).

Graduate Course Projects

  • The python script (Crank-Nicolson Option Pricing in Python) uses Crank-Nicolson finite difference method to evalaute price of European call option, with risk free interest rate of 5%, volatility of 30%, stricke price of $100 and initial price of the underlying asset as $80. (PDF version with plot: Crank-Nicolson Option Pricing In Python - PDF). The MATLAB version of the same method is attached as PDf version (Crank-Nicolson Option Pricing in MATLAB - PDF). We can see that the same model can be constructed with simpler code structure, giving us the same results. The Black-Scholes conditions are considered to write these scripts.
